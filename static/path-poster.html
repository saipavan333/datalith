The Golden Path to Data Engineer

~83h core
28 tracks · pick one platform
≈ 8–10 weeks @ 1–1.5h/day
  1. 1
    Foundations of Data · 1.8h
    The vocabulary & mental models everything builds on — role, data shapes, formats, OLTP vs OLAP, the lifecycle, data quality.
  2. 2
    Databases & SQL · 11.8h
    DBMS → RDBMS → SQL to fluency (joins, windows, transactions, indexing) → NoSQL. The most-used skill on the job — over-invest.
  3. 3
    Python + Data Libraries + Unix · 14.5h
    The language & engineering practices, then NumPy / pandas / Arrow / Polars / DuckDB + SQLAlchemy / Pydantic / ingestion. What you build with.
  4. 4
    Data Modeling & Warehousing · 4.8h
    Turn requirements into schemas that scale — dimensional modeling, star schemas, SCD, grain, the semantic layer.
  5. 5
    Big Data · Pipelines · Streaming · Lakehouse · 13.7h
    The heart of the job — Spark & internals, reliable orchestrated pipelines, real-time streaming, and the lakehouse (Delta/Iceberg, medallion).
  6. 6
    Pick ONE cloud platform · ~7h · the branch
    Learn Cloud DE (provider-agnostic) first, then go deep on the one your target jobs use. Skim a second later.
    Databricks 7.7h · Spark/lakehouse shops Snowflake 6.8h · warehouse-first AWS 8.7h · most common GCP 6.1h · BigQuery shops
  7. 7
    Operations & Quality · 6.4h
    DataOps (Git, CI/CD, IaC, containers, observability, contracts + the DE craft & career), Governance & Security, Performance. Makes you production-grade.
  8. 8
    System Design + Interview Bank · 10.1h
    The design method + distributed-systems fundamentals + worked designs, then 16 company playbooks & round banks. Start ~3–4 weeks pre-interview.
  9. ✓
    Capstone Projects · 4.2h
    Build end-to-end projects that prove the whole path. This is your portfolio.

Companions — weave in, don't block on

DSA (2.8h) alongside Python, revisit pre-interview · Data Viz & Diagrams (2.3h) from step 5 · Cheat sheets — quick reference, skim before interviews.

Optional specializations

ML for DE (3.1h) & Agentic AI (2.6h) — take these if your target role touches ML pipelines, feature stores, or RAG/LLM data work.
